Reading should feel easy. This page lets you set up how text looks across the web. Use the panel on the right to try different settings. The words you are reading right now will change to show you what your choices look like.

When you find a setup that feels comfortable, click Save and Start. Your settings will be applied to every website you visit.

What is dyslexia?

Dyslexia is a difference in how the brain processes written words. It affects about one in five people. Letters can seem to move, flip, or blur. Reading can take more effort than it does for others.

This is not a measure of how smart you are. Many people with dyslexia are highly creative and fast thinkers. The words just need a little help to stay still.

How Pegasus View helps.

Pegasus View changes the font, text size, line spacing, and letter spacing on any website. These changes can make words easier to tell apart and sentences easier to follow from start to finish.

Some fonts are designed to help readers with dyslexia. Wider spacing between lines gives each row room to breathe. More space between letters stops them from crowding together.

Small changes can make a big difference. Try the settings on the right and see what feels best for you. There is no wrong answer.

A note on colours.

A pale yellow or cream background can be easier on the eyes than a bright white page. Dark blue or dark brown text on a warm background reduces glare and makes words stand out more clearly.

Use the colour pickers to find a combination that feels restful. The background colour of this entire page will change when you pick a new one.
